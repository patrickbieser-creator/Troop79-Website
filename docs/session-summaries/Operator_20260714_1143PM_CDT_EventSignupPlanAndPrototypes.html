What Was Accomplished

Research

Surveyed scouting platforms (TroopTrack, TroopWebHost, Scoutbook, SOAR, TroopMaster), commercial signup UX (SignUpGenius, Evite, Eventbrite), and BSA program rules (Guide to Safe Scouting, YP FAQs, AHMR). Two competitive gaps no platform fills became first-class features: driver coordination with seat counts and waitlists. Validated pain points shaped the design: hard deadlines, permission-slip tracking separate from RSVP, per-patrol headcounts, and no-login friction (login walls kill response rates).

Plan — Plans/Event-Signup.md (v3, Active)

  • Composable blocks, not per-type templates (D-024): 8 blocks — content, attendance, pricing, capacity+waitlist, drivers, shifts/tasks, questions, compliance flags — composed per event; a 13-type preset matrix seeds defaults; one generic /events/[id] renderer. New event shapes need zero new code.
  • Pricing tiers (D-025): labeled tiers per event (Scout / Webelos / Adult-skiing / Adult-chaperone $0), per-event or per-day (summer camp adult $40/day); owed always derived; no online payments — instructions + leader "payment received" checkbox.
  • Participation kinds (D-026): full / driver-only (per-leg driving with per-leg seats, never charged) / contributor (donates pancake mix or OJ without attending). Money donations stay outside signup.
  • Family identity (D-027): shared FAMILY_PASSWORD gate + household picker for Phase 1; explicit accepted risk; magic links arrive with Phase 4 per-user auth.
  • Three tech-lead reviews (v1, v2 delta, v3 delta) — every required change folded in: identity CHECKs, partial unique upsert indexes, FOR UPDATE capacity/claim RPCs, cancelled-entries-release-slots, per-leg seat columns, tier-deletion RESTRICT.
