The Problem

Identity was inferred on every page load. A human had no single record — the same person could be a scouts row, a leaders row, and one scout_parents row per child, with nothing linking them. households.ts reconstructed who was who by normalising names and email addresses.

That inference failed twice in one week: first when two siblings' records spelled a parent's name differently (“JamieLynn” / “Jamie Lynn”), then for four adults recorded by nickname on one record and formal name on the other (“Dan”/“Daniel”, “Mike”/“Michael”, “Nate”/“Nathaniel”). Each listed a real person twice in the public signup picker and would have let them sign up twice for one event.

16 of 42 leaders were also in scout_parents. String matching was load-bearing for 38% of the adult roster.

What Was Accomplished

The Identity Spine

people plus household_members, person_roles and relationships, with nullable person_id links on the three existing tables. Deliberately additive: scouts.id and leaders.code stay as business keys, so 9,723 ledger rows and the rename-cascade machinery were never touched. The backfill matched on exact email only — fuzzy name matching is the mechanism that caused the bugs, so it was not used at any confidence level.

Verified against production: zero unlinked records anywhere, zero links pointing at a merged-away person, and the temporary correlation column cleaned up. Re-running the migration is a complete no-op.

Three Axes That Were Previously One

Role is what someone does in the troop. Relationship is how they relate to another person. Status is whether they are still around. Parent is a relationship, not a role — so “not a leader, not a counselor, only a parent” is a person with zero role rows, and absence is the statement rather than a flag.

This is what made a guardian at a different address expressible for the first time, and what lets an aged-out scout keep their siblings and their household while ceasing to be a scout.

Roster Import

A 125-row roster CSV staged into import_batches / import_rows / merge_suggestions, matched exact-only, and reviewed by hand: 124 accepted, 47 superseded, 1 rejected, with 42 people created who were not previously on record. Nothing was written to live tables without an explicit accept.

Roster Rework

Four tabs — Active Scouts, Inactive Scouts, Leaders, Adults — driven by a single person_directory view so the Roster, the signup picker and the login pool cannot drift apart. Tab membership is derived from current role, and there is deliberately no action that sets it. A person editor manages household, roles and relationships; the scout editor records parents as relationships rather than free text while keeping the at-a-glance phone and email layout.

Household Manager

Households were only reachable through a person, so duplicates and empties were invisible. Production had two “Stollenwerk” households (two genuinely separate families), two “Haslam” and two “Pasquesi”. Labels are deliberately not forced unique — two Johnson families may legitimately share a surname — so each household now lists its members and repeated names are flagged.

Bugs Caught Before They Shipped

Several of the session's fixes came from watching the new tooling misbehave rather than from reading the diff:

  • A merge suggester that manufactured the error it existed to prevent. A shared-surname rule produced 73 candidates against 102 people and proposed merging Jack Porter into his father Jason, and Anjali Sankpal-Tatera into her sister Maya. Removed; the narrowed view returns 11.
  • Exact-email matching proposed merging children into their parents. Families share one address, so the matcher offered Ben Bieser → Daniel Bieser and Summer Curtis → her daughter Fiona Kimble. Email matches now require name corroboration.
  • A backfill that correlated by name — inside the migration whose whole purpose was to end name-correlation. Caught by qa-lead and by a from-scratch rebuild; the first attempted fix was itself wrong and only surfaced because counts were compared against a known-good run.
  • A data-loss trap in the scout form. replaceParents() wipes before re-inserting and an absent field defaulted to an empty array — so a form that simply stopped sending parents would have silently deleted every parent row on the scout being saved.

Blockers Resolved

The family gate verification was blocked by 10 duplicate people. Switching the picker to identity-based matching would have listed each of them twice. All 10 were merged through the new tool, and households.ts now resolves 83 adults with zero duplicates — up from 41, because the 42 import-created people had no legacy record and were invisible until the loader stopped requiring one.

Decisions

D-042 — People identity spine: identity declared, not inferred.
D-043 — Role and relationship are different things, and both are separate from status.
D-044 — Age-out is not inactivity; at 18 a scout is no longer a scout.
D-045 — Imports are staged and human-accepted, never auto-merged.
D-046 — Household labels are not unique; membership disambiguates them.
D-047 — signup_entries records one participant identity.

Quality Gate

A qa-lead review before the production migration returned ship-with-fixes, 7/10: access control clean at all four layers, but two correctness bugs in the identity-linking logic itself. All four findings were fixed before anything reached production — the name-correlated backfill, a non-atomic accept that could change a person while reporting failure, a non-idempotent role insert, and a missing undo runbook.
