Event Registration  ·  Design Prototypes

Event Registration System

Sign-ups for everything the troop does — campouts, service projects, fundraisers, outings, merit badge clinics, Courts of Honor — designed so one parent answers for the whole family in about a minute on a phone. Payments stay offline: the site shows the amount and how to pay, and leaders tick off who has. The event category decides which questions appear, so a Court of Honor never asks about driver seats.

Public · Family flow

Campout signup

The flagship 4-state flow: family password → pick your scouts → per-person Going/Not going with drivers & dietary → confirmation with edit-until-deadline. Includes fee display and offline payment instructions.

 Public · Shift slots

Fundraiser shifts

Named slots with hard capacity — scout wash crews and adult supervisors per 2-hour shift. Full shifts flip to a waitlist. The SignUpGenius pattern, on our own site.

Court of Honor RSVP

Whole-family headcount including a plain guest number (no names needed for grandma), plus potluck dish slots. No campout machinery in sight.

Event roster

Headcount tiles, patrol-grouped roster, dietary rollup, seats-vs-riders matrix, two-deep & female-leader banners, health-form/YPT flags, waitlist promotion, paid checkboxes — and it prints for the campsite binder.

The design in nine rules

  1. Households, not individuals. Picking your scout(s) defines your family; parents come along automatically from records on file. (Scoutbook’s per-person-only RSVP is its most complained-about gap.)
  2. Going / Not going / No response. No “Maybe.” Silence stays visible so leaders know exactly who to chase.
  3. The category shapes the form. Campouts ask drivers + dietary; family events ask a guest count; fundraisers offer shift slots; meetings ask nothing extra.
  4. Deadlines carry their reason. “Sign up by May 9 so the grubmaster can shop” — then self-service locks and leaders can still edit.
  5. Drivers are event-level data. Direction + open seats per adult, rolled into a seats-vs-riders check both directions.
  6. Money stays offline. The site displays amounts and instructions; leaders tick “paid.” No cards, ever.
  7. Flag, never block. Expired health forms and YPT show as warnings on the leader roster — a signup is never rejected over paperwork.
  8. Safety is computed. Two-deep (2 adults 21+) and female-leader coverage are derived from the going list and surfaced as banners.
  9. Signups become records. The roster seeds post-event attendance, which seeds camping nights / service hours / activity ledger entries (Phase 3).

What ships when

  • Phase 1: event detail pages, family signup flow, capacity + waitlist, leader roster with print — the campout and Court of Honor prototypes.
  • Phase 2: shift/dish slots, electronic permission slips, email confirmations & reminders — the fundraiser prototype.
  • Phase 3: roster → attendance → ledger conversion; summer camp / high adventure commitment + payment-milestone tracking; MB clinic session caps.

Open questions for review

  • Separate FAMILY_PASSWORD (recommended) vs. reusing the scout password?
  • Who sees the who’s-going list — counts-only when signed out, names for signed-in families, details for leaders?
  • Guests as a plain number (as prototyped) vs. named rows?
