Troop 79 Wins Council Spirit Award at Fall Camporee

Fourteen scouts and five patrols swept the leadership and teamwork stations at Cub & Scout Camporee, bringing home the council’s top spirit trophy for the first time since 2019.

By Scoutmaster Mindy Stollenwerk · March 30, 2026 · 6 min read

Closing campfire, Saturday night. Patrol Raven leads the flag ceremony after being named top overall patrol for the weekend.

Troop 79 arrived at Camp Indian Trail with five patrols and one goal: end a six-year drought at the district’s biggest fall event. By Saturday afternoon, that goal was history — the troop claimed first place in orienteering, second in knot relay, and the overall Spirit Award, a trophy that hasn’t hung in our meeting room since 2019.

A Weekend of Team Building

Friday night set the tone. Instead of the usual free-camp chaos, senior patrol leader Marcus D. organized a lights-out flashlight orienteering course through the west woods, splitting scouts into mixed-age teams on purpose — a call that paid off Saturday morning when those same teams anchored the troop’s station rotations.

“We told the new Scouts on day one: nobody sits out,” said assistant scoutmaster Dana Okafor, who coordinated the troop’s camporee logistics. “That’s really what the spirit award is about — not just who wins stations, but who shows up for each other in between.”

Sunday’s closing ceremony brought the full council together for the awards announcement. When Troop 79’s number was called for overall spirit, the reaction from our scouts was immediate — a decade of camporees, and this is the one they’ll remember.
