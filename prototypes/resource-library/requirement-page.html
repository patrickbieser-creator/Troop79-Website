Resource Library· First Class· Requirement 9

9a

Visit and discuss citizenship with a community leader

Visit and have a conversation with an elected official or civic leader in your community — then be ready to discuss what you learned about their role and your own responsibilities as a citizen.

Paraphrased — confirm exact wording against the current handbook at sign-off.

This is the requirement scouts put off the longest — and the one they end up telling stories about. You don’t need an appointment with the mayor: an alderperson, a school-board member, a village trustee, or a judge all count, and most are genuinely happy to talk to a scout who emails them.

Our troop’s usual path: look up your alder using the link below, send a short email (there’s a template in the resources), and bring three questions from the question list. Afterward, jot down what surprised you — that’s exactly what a leader will ask about at sign-off. Several scouts have done this one together as a patrol visit; that’s fine, but each scout has their own conversation notes.
